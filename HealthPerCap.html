Health per capita compare to the GDP per capita for the G-20 Nations from 2000 to 2018 [Infographic]

Renzo Castagnino

Data Scientist - The George Washington University

In the following graphs we will show the map of the health per capita by region; and how the health per capita is compared to the GDP per capita for all of the G-20 countries from the year 2000 to 2018. In the map we can see that the US has the highest health expenditure per capita ($7600 for 2018), followed by Germany, Canada and Australia. If we observe the scatter plot, we can see as the USA with the highest percentage of GDP per Capita, followed by a cluster of european countries and Australia. The lowest countries on the rank are India and Indonesia.

From the chart we can also observe how the GPD per capita is compared to the health per capita. Again, for the US, in 2018 we have a health per capita of about $10,000, followed by Australia and Germany with $5300 and $5290 respectively.South Africa, Indonesia and India are the lowest in the rank.

Average Health Per Capita vs Average GDP Per Capita - 2000 to 2018

In terms of the average health per capita, there is a constant upward trend. In the year 2000, the avergage health per capita was around 1000 USD, but for the year 2018, it was almost 2700 USD.
